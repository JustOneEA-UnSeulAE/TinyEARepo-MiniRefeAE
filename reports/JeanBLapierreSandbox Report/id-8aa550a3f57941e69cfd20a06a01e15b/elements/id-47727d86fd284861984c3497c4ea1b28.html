4. Responsible & Ethical Use [Principle] ()
4. Responsible & Ethical Use Statement BDM upholds responsible and ethical use of data, respecting privacy, legal obligations, and societal values. Ethical data use is essential to maintaining public trust and delivering fair, transparent, and client-centric programs. Rationale 1. Builds and sustains trust with citizens, clients, and stakeholders 2. Ensures compliance with laws, regulations, and departmental policies (e.g., Privacy Act, Values and Ethics Code) 3. Prevents misuse, bias, or unintended consequences in program delivery and decision-making 4. Supports fairness, transparency, and accountability in how data informs services and outcomes 5. Reinforces the importance of respecting individual rights and cultural sensitivities in data use 6. Encourages responsible innovation while managing risks associated with automation and analytics 7. Aligns with public sector values of integrity, respect, and service excellence Implications 1. BDM will apply ethical frameworks, including privacy impact assessments and fairness reviews, to guide data use in programs and services 2. Program teams will be responsible for identifying and mitigating ethical risks in how data is collected, interpreted, and applied 3. Data governance processes will include ethical oversight, especially for high-impact or sensitive data use cases 4. Training and awareness initiatives will promote a culture of ethical data stewardship across BDM 5. BDM will ensure that data use reflects client-centric values, avoids discrimination, and supports equitable service delivery 6. Ethical considerations will be embedded in program design, evaluation, and continuous improvement
