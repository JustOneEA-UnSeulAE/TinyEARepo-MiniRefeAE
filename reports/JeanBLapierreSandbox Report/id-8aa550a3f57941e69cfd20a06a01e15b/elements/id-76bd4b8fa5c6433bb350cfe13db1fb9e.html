Data visualization process - Text version 1 – Plan Document business and data requirements Identify audience Consider privacy Identify data stewards Obtain access to the data Document business and data requirements: Clearly define the purpose and objectives. Identify key questions or problems your visualization products aims to address or represent. Determine the expected outcomes and how the visualization will support decision-making. Identify audience: Understand the needs, expertise, and expectations of your target audience (e.g., senior executives, analysts, public). Consider the level of technical knowledge and familiarity with data interpretation. Tailor visualization product complexity, interactivity, and details accordingly. Consider privacy: As outlined in Section 8, privacy must be considered from the outset of any data visualization project. Identify data stewards: Identify data stewards who have the data, and decision-makers responsible for validations and approvals. Establish accountability for maintaining and updating the visualization products if needed. Obtain access to data: Identify data sources and secure necessary permissions to access data. Assess key stakeholder requirements to validate data quality, completeness, and reliability with data steward before proceeding to next step.
