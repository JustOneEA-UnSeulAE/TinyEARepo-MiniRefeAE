11. AI Ready [Principle] ()
11. AI Ready Statement BDM prepares and manages data to support responsible and effective use of advanced analytics and artificial intelligence. This includes ensuring data is structured, contextualized, and governed in ways that enable automation, insight generation, and innovation in program delivery. Rationale 1. Prepares data for emerging technologies and analytical use cases, including AI, machine learning, and automation 2. Enhances discoverability and contextual understanding of data to support intelligent decision-making 3. Enables semantic integration and reasoning by ensuring data is meaningfully linked and interpretable 4. Aligns with Government of Canada Directive on Automated Decision-Making 5. Supports program modernization by enabling predictive analytics, personalization, and operational efficiency 6. Reinforces the importance of governance, transparency, and ethical considerations in AI-enabled environments 7. Encourages collaboration between program teams, data stewards, and analytics experts Implications 1. BDM will invest in metadata, ontologies, and semantic models to support AI-readiness and contextual understanding 2. Data will be structured and documented to enable machine-readable formats and intelligent processing 3. Governance frameworks will include standards for AI use, including transparency, fairness, and accountability 4. Program teams will collaborate with data science and analytics experts to identify opportunities for responsible AI adoption 5. AI readiness will be treated as a strategic capability, supporting innovation and improved service delivery 6. Data used in AI applications will be subject to ethical review, privacy impact assessments, and continuous monitoring 7. BDM will provision data in such a way that bias in AI models is minimized 8. BDM will undertake staffing on AI ethics
