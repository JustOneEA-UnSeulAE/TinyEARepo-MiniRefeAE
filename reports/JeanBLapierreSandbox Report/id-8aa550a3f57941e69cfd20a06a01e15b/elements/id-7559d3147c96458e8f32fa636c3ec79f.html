1.8 There are only three basic concepts of data architecture: entity, association and attribute DESCRIPTION a) Entities and Associations: A model comprises a collection of things about which information can be known. These things - business concepts - are of two fundamental types: actors - things that may act and react, called entities, and the actions and interactions of those actors, called associations. b) Entities and associations can be described with qualities called attributes, e.g. the weight or color of an entity, or the duration or type of an association. RATIONALE • All data models reduce to these fundamental constructs. • Simplicity improves consistency and comprehension. IMPLICATION • Avoid introducing non-standard modeling primitives. • Classify every concept explicitly as entity, association, or attribute. • Use this triad to resolve modeling disputes.
