10. Reusable [Principle] ()
10. Reusable Statement BDM supports the preparation and maintenance of in ways that enable its reuse across multiple programs, services, and contexts. Reusability focuses on making data accessible, understandable, and fit for purpose—regardless of how it was originally acquired. Rationale 1. Promotes efficiency by reducing duplication of effort across programs and initiatives 2. Supports consistency and standardization in how data is interpreted and applied 3. Enables scalable and agile program delivery by leveraging existing data assets 4. Reduces time and cost for future initiatives by building on trusted, well-documented data 5. Encourages a shift from one-off data use to sustainable, service-oriented data practices 6. Aligns with Government of Canada goals for data stewardship, openness, and innovation Implications 1. BDM will develop and maintain reusable data products and services, with clear ownership and documentation 2. Metadata, definitions, and contextual information will be provided to support discoverability and usability 3. Data will be cleaned, standardized, and versioned to ensure it can be reliably reused across different use cases 4. Governance processes will support the validation, certification, and lifecycle management of reusable data assets 5. Program teams will be encouraged to design data outputs with reuse in mind, even if the data was not originally collected by them 6. Reusability will be treated as a strategic capability, enabling innovation and cross-program collaboration 7. This principle focuses on how data is prepared and maintained, complementing the “Collected once, Use Multiple” principle, which focuses on data capture once and reuse multiple times.
