Reduced inequalities (SDG 10) We'll help young people, aged 15 to 30 years, especially those facing barriers to employment, including youth with disabilities, Indigenous youth and racialized youth. Through the Youth Employment and Skills Strategy, diverse groups of youth will be provided with tailored support and skills training so they can successfully enter the labour market. This work also supports SDG 8 (Decent work and economic growth) The Black-led Philanthropic Endowment Fund provides sustainable funding and self-sustaining resources to Black-led, Black-focused, and Black-serving organizations in Canada. Led by Black communities, the fund will support projects that combat anti-Black racism and improve social and economic outcomes for Black communities The Supporting Black Canadian Communities Initiative (SBCCI) will help us reduce inequalities. Funding will be provided to the Canadian Institute for People of Afrikan Ancestry. This organization will help Canada's efforts to tackle racism and discrimination by serving as a centre for research, knowledge, and community engagement. In addition, SBCCI provides funding for specific needs identified by Black communities
