8. Transparency on Use [Principle] ()
8. Transparency on Use Statement BDM promotes data usage that is transparent, traceable, and clearly communicated to stakeholders and citizens. Transparency fosters trust, supports accountability, and reinforces responsible data stewardship in program delivery. Rationale 1. Builds public trust in how data is used to inform and deliver government programs 2. Supports compliance with the Privacy Act, Access to Information Act, and departmental transparency policies 3. Enables auditability and oversight of data practices across programs 4. Reinforces accountability for data use decisions and their impact on clients 5. Promotes ethical and responsible stewardship of data throughout its lifecycle 6. Aligns with Government of Canada commitments to openness, integrity, and citizen engagement 7. Helps clients understand how their data is used, contributing to informed consent and confidence Implications 1. BDM will maintain clear records of data access and usage, including who accessed what data, when, and for what purpose 2. Data usage policies will be documented, accessible, and regularly reviewed to ensure clarity and compliance 3. Logging and audit capabilities will be implemented to support oversight and accountability 4. Communication strategies will include transparency commitments, such as privacy notices, consent language, and public reporting 5. Program teams will be responsible for ensuring that data use is explainable and defensible, especially in client-facing services 6. Transparency will be embedded in governance, training, and continuous improvement efforts
