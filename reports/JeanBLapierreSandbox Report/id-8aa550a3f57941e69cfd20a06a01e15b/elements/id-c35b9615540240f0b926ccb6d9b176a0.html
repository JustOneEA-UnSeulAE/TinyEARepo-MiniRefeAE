No poverty (SDG 1) Old Age Security (OAS) will help reduce the incidence of low income among seniors. OAS benefits will be adjusted four times a year (in January, April, July and October), to match changes in the cost of living. The Guaranteed Income Supplement will also help to reduce poverty among seniors The Canada Child Benefit (https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/child-family-benefits/canada-child-benefit-overview.html) supports families with the costs of raising children under 18, focusing on those most in need. It provides tax-free monthly payments to eligible families, and since July 2018, has been adjusted, to match the rising cost of living. We'll continue to support the Department of Finance and the Canada Revenue Agency on the Canada Child Benefit Through the Community Outreach and Liaison Service (COLS), we'll help excluded and underserved populations access financial support and services. By proactively identifying and removing barriers to benefit uptake, COLS promotes social inclusion and ensures that vulnerable communities can fully participate in Canada's social safety net
