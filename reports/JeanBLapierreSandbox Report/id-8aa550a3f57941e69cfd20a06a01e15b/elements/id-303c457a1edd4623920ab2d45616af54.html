P6 - Data as an Asset Statement Data is a strategic enterprise asset that must be ethically collected, high‑quality, well‑governed, and shared across the organization to support business outcomes, decision‑making, and effective citizen and business‑centric service delivery. Rationale Data underpins all digital services and decision‑making and holds measurable enterprise value. Treating data as a shared, strategic asset—rather than an application‑specific by‑product—enables privacy‑by‑design, interoperability, reuse, and long‑term architectural flexibility. A data‑first approach ensures technology solutions are designed around data, allowing EA to trace data usage, privacy, and quality requirements back to business and client needs. Implications Data must be collected once, meet defined quality, ethical, and privacy standards, and align with recognized international standards. Clear accountability must be established through designated data owners, stewards, and custodians. Authoritative (primary) data assets must be protected for integrity and authenticity; secondary data assets (e.g., data lakes, warehouses, extracts) must remain traceable to their sources. Data should be logically decoupled from applications, enabling reuse, interoperability, modernization, and migration without disrupting core data assets. EA teams must adopt a data‑first mindset, shifting from application‑centric designs to shared, enterprise‑wide data architectures.
