P3 - Digital First Statement Services and communications must be designed and optimized for delivery through secure, responsive digital channels, while ensuring inclusive, supported alternatives remain available for clients who need them. Rationale Clients expect government services to be easy-to-use, secure and accessible online from the information and application stage up to a decision on their file. Digital First approach improves service quality, efficiency, scalability, and resilience, while supporting increased digital uptake and business transformation. Implications Digital channels are the default and preferred means for delivering services and communications. Services must be designed to enable end‑to‑end digital self‑service, minimizing manual, paper‑based, or in‑person interactions where appropriate. Business processes should be redesigned to fully leverage digital capabilities, enabling operational efficiency and scalability. Digital ownership and decision‑making responsibility should be embedded within the business, supported by IM/IT. Traditional channels are maintained where required but should be aligned with and supported by digital back‑end processes. Duplication across channels should be reduced through shared platforms, automation, and reusable digital components. Solutions must maintain a single channel of engagement ensuring a seamless, predictable experience and reduces friction caused by fragmented interactions
