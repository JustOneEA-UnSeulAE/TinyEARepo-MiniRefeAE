1.11 Representations are not entities DESCRIPTION Representations are collections of data, captured to a common medium, for example, an application captured on paper or digitally. Such representations are business concepts, but not entities from a data architecture perspective. They are a snapshot collection of data about one or more entities and associations, manifested through a common medium. In essence, these are information packets created to communicate information in business meaningful transactions. The capture of information in this form is business line specific. It is typically necessary to both capture the representation as a record of the communicated business transaction, and to deconstruct that representation into the underlying entities and attributes. It may also be necessary to construct representations separately from the underlying data, for example, a worksheet used to calculate an intermediate result. Care should be taken to ensure that representations are not confused with the underlying entities. RATIONALE • Forms, files, and messages are containers, not business objects. • Treating representations as entities obscures real-world meaning. IMPLICATION • Capture representations as records when required. • Decompose representations into underlying entities and attributes. • Prevent reuse of representation structures as master data.
