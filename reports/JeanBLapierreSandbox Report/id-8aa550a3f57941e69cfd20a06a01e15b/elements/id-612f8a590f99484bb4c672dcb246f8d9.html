Gender equality (SDG 5) We'll continue to fund projects that recruit, retain, and help women apprentices succeed in eligible Red Seals trades through our Canadian Apprenticeship Strategy Women in the Skilled Trades Initiative. These trades are mainly in construction and manufacturing, where women are underrepresented. More than 11,500 women are expected to benefit over the next five years (2023-24 to 2027-28) Employment Insurance maternity benefits will continue to help women who are away from work due to pregnancy and childbirth. We'll also continue to ensure that parental benefits support parents caring for their child and help to empower women economically Employment Insurance benefits will also continue for caregivers who take time off work to support adult family members or children who are critically ill or injured, or who need end-of-life care. As noted in the 2023 to 2024 Employment Insurance Monitoring and Assessment Report, women make up approximately two thirds of the claimants for these benefits. These benefits help to recognize the importance and value of caregiving and domestic work
