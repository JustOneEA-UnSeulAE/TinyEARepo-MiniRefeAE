Decent work and economic growth (SDG 8) The Canadian Apprenticeship Strategy - Union Training and Innovation Program - Sustainable Jobs Stream will allow us to help unions develop green skills training for workers in the Red Seal trades. Approximately 29,300 apprentices and journeypersons are expected to benefit between 2025-26 and 2028-29 Our Labour Market Agreements will continue to advance SDG 8-as well as SDG 4 (Quality education) and SDG 10 (Reduced inequalities) by improving access to skills training and employment supports. Workers will get help to upgrade their skills for a changing labour market The Workforce Development Agreements will continue to help people who are under-represented in the labour market (for example: persons with disabilities, Indigenous peoples, and recent immigrants). Supports provided through these agreements will help them prepare for, find, and keep jobs. Participants usually come from groups facing higher poverty rates. These supports also advance SDG 4 (Quality education) and SDG 10 (Reduced inequalities) With our Enabling Fund for Official Language Minority Communities, we'll help official language minority communities (OLMCs) grow and thrive. We'll fund organizations to build partnerships, improve their community economic and human resources development, and deliver employment assistance services. These activities will support the economic vitality of OLMCs and help people in these communities Through the Indigenous Skills and Employment Training Program, we'll contribute to this SDG and the cross-cutting objectives of Canada's implementation of the 2030 Agenda, including the objectives of leaving no one behind and advancing reconciliation with Indigenous peoples. We'll fund 119 Indigenous organizations to provide skills and employment training at over 650 points of service nationwide. This will help First Nations, Inuit, Métis as well Urban/Unaffiliated Indigenous peoples develop skills and find jobs. Organizations can create programs that fit their community's needs (for example: supports such as childcare) to help people participate in training and employment Our Sustainable Jobs Training Fund will support 8 projects to help over 10,000 workers upgrade or gain new skills for jobs in the low-carbon economy We'll continue to administer and enforce the measures introduced in 2023-24 to strengthen the Temporary Foreign Worker (TFW) Program's integrity and curb suspected misuse of the program, promoting decent and productive work, and sustainable economic growth We'll continue to strengthen the TFW Program compliance regime by conducting inspections, reviewing tips and allegations within two business days, and collaborating with federal, provincial, and territorial partners to hold employers accountable and protect both the Canadian labour market and the rights of temporary foreign workers We'll continue to work with the International Labour Organization to develop, implement, and promote international labour standards on issues like violence and harassment, health and safety, and challenges for people working on digital labour platforms. Our goal is to ensure minimum labour standards are met globally and provide a level playing field for workers and businesses in Canada We'll keep working with the United Nations, and the Organization of American States, through the Inter-American Conference of Ministers of Labour, and the Inter-American Network for Labour Administration to promote international labour standards and fundamental workplace rights Our work in support of this SDG, includes a focus on target 8.7 which concerns eliminating forced and child labour. We'll conduct research on forced labour in global supply chains, affecting groups like women, children and youth, low-income individuals, informal workers, Indigenous Peoples, racialized individuals, persons with disabilities, immigrants, and 2SLGBTQI+ individuals. We'll also continue to provide technical assistance to trade partners, to promote labour rights and reduce labour exploitation globally Work will continue to address the misclassification of employees in the road transportation industry. This includes raising awareness and enforcing rules to ensure federally regulated employees receive the benefits they are entitled to
