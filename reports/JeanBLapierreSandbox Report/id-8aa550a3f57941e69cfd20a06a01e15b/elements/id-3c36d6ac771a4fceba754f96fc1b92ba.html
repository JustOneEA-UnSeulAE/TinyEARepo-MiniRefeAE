P2 - Client-Centric [Principle] Deliver high-quality, timely and accurate services that are responsive to current and emerging client needs and to continue to innovate and meet new standards of excellence ()
P2 - Client-Centric Statement Deliver high-quality, timely and accurate services that are responsive to current and emerging client needs and to continue to innovate and meet new standards of excellence. Rationale Clients expect government services to be integrated, intuitive, accessible, and responsive to their individual circumstances. A client‑centric approach improves service outcomes, trust, and efficiency by focusing on real user needs rather than organizational structures or internal constraints. Implications Services must be built around client needs (e.g., multi-channel). Clients must be provided with a “one-stop service” that is simple and intuitive to navigate. Clients must be able to access services anytime, anywhere, and on any device, where appropriate. Clients must be engaged and involved in the design and development of services. Client and employee feedback data must be collected and analyzed to improve services and inform implementation. Services must be delivered across multiple channels using modern architectures. Client services must have clearly defined service levels. Services must be measured to improve performance. An ESDC strategy must be leveraged or established for common citizen and business data sets (e.g. name, address) and common client applications (e.g. authentication, payments, notifications). Shared data and platforms must be used to enable seamless, integrated, and personalized client experiences while respecting privacy and security requirements.
