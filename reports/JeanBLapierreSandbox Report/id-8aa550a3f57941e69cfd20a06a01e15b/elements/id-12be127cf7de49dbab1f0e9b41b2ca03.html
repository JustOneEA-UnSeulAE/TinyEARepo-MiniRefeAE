P4 - Strategically Aligned Statement Enterprise architecture decisions and initiatives must directly support the mandate and strategic objectives of ESDC, ensuring that resources are focused on outcomes that deliver the greatest value. Rationale A business‑led, strategically focused architecture enables ESDC to achieve its mandate, deliver on strategic priorities, and adapt to evolving client and stakeholder needs. Aligning architecture decisions with mandate and strategy ensures optimal return on investment and prevents effort being diverted toward solutions that do not advance core organizational outcomes. Implications Architectures must clearly demonstrate how business capabilities, services and processes support organizational values and strategic goals. Architecture must reflect a holistic, end‑to‑end vision that is driven by business needs rather than technology alone. As new needs emerge, priorities must be reassessed and adjusted proportionally based on impact to the mandate and strategy. Architecture provides a holistic, end‑to‑end view of how the organization operates to deliver outcomes to Canadians. Priorities must be established collectively, for the benefit of the entire organization—not individual teams or silos. The department must be able to assess the impact of changes in policies, standards, and directives on its ability to deliver its mandate. Enterprise architectures must describe both the current state and the target state of the department. Architecture must enable the identification of gaps between current and target states, informing prioritization, sequencing, and investment decisions. Architectures must also highlight barriers to transformation and provide recommendations to address and overcome those barriers. The department must be able to assess and understand the impact of changes in policy, standards, and directives on its ability to deliver on its mandate.
