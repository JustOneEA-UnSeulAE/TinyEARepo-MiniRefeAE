7. Meaningful and Trusted [Principle] ()
7. Meaningful and Trusted Statement BDM guarantees that data is meaningful, accurate, and trusted by its users. High-quality data supports confident decision-making, effective program delivery, and improved outcomes for clients and stakeholders. Rationale 1. Enables evidence-based decisions that are timely, relevant, and defensible 2. Builds confidence in data-driven services and program performance 3. Reduces the risk of misinterpretation, errors, and unintended consequences 4. Enhances the credibility and value of data used in reporting, evaluation, and service design 5. Supports transparency and accountability in how data informs public programs 6. Encourages continuous improvement through feedback and validation 7. Aligns with public expectations for reliable and trustworthy government data Implications 1. BDM will implement data quality management practices, including profiling, validation, and cleansing 2. Program teams will be responsible for ensuring data is fit for purpose, relevant, and interpretable 3. Metadata, definitions, and contextual information will be provided to support accurate use and understanding 4. Feedback mechanisms will be established to identify and address data quality issues over time 5. Data quality will be monitored and reported as part of program performance and governance 6. Trusted data will be prioritized in decision-making, service delivery, and public reporting
