Ima and her colleagues explored practical approaches to protecting critical data for programs like Employment Insurance, such as creating safe environments within the Government to test and validate AI solutions before they go live. Ima reminded audience members that public servants are accountable to Canadians, and that we must protect their rights and earn their trust. That trust, she emphasized, depends on transparency: “Trust cannot be assumed. It must be earned through openness and co-creation with communities.” Ima added that we need to provide Canadians with clear information about what data we collect, how we use it, and to understand the realistic limits of AI to avoid overpromising and underdelivering.
