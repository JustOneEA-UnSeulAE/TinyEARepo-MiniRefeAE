1. Treat Data as an Asset [Principle] ()
1. Treat Data as an Asset Statement BDM treats data as a strategic asset that must be actively managed and governed to deliver measurable value to clients, stakeholders, and citizens. This approach supports more responsive, informed, and efficient program delivery. Rationale 1. Recognizes data as a foundational enabler of modern service delivery and program transformation 2. Supports evidence-based decision-making, policy development, and performance measurement 3. Encourages proactive investment in data governance, stewardship, and infrastructure 4. Reinforces accountability for data stewardship and lifecycle management across business units 5. Promotes a cultural shift toward data-informed operations and continuous improvement 6. Aligns with Government of Canada priorities for digital government, transparency, and client-centric services 7. Elevates data to the same level of strategic importance as financial and human resources in decision-making and planning 8. Enables collaboration across programs and jurisdictions by fostering shared understanding and trust in data Implications 1. Data will be governed with clear stewardship, and lifecycle management 2. Stewardship roles will be formally assigned within business units to ensure accountability for data quality, relevance, and fitness for operational use 3. Data lifecycle management will be embedded in program operations, from acquisition to archival or secure disposal 4. Data investments will be prioritized based on business value 5. Decision-making will factor in the strategic importance, risk, and reuse potential of data assets. 6. Data will be integrated into program planning, risk assessments, and performance frameworks to support continuous improvement In BDM terms, a data asset is any piece of data that supports BDM Program, such as program delivery, compliance, and reporting. It is raw or structured information that is collected, stored, and managed for operational, analytical, or strategic purposes. The key difference between data asset and data product is that data product is more a more finished product with documentation and a level of support, whereas data asset is more for internal use and may not come with as much documentation and support. Characteristics: Can be raw, processed, or aggregated data Includes databases, datasets, records, logs, metadata, etc. May be used across multiple systems or programs Often managed through governance, stewardship, and lifecycle policies Not necessarily packaged for end-user consumption Examples: A client database Employment statistics Transaction logs Metadata catalog
