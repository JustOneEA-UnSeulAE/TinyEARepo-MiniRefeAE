2. Available for Use Statement BDM ensures guarantees that data is accessible to authorized employees and partners when needed, in a secure, timely, and efficient manner. Ensuring availability supports responsive program delivery, informed decision-making, and improved client outcomes. Rationale 1. Enables timely access to data for frontline services, program operations, and decision-makers 2. Reduces delays and bottlenecks in service delivery and reporting 3. Supports operational agility and responsiveness to client needs 4. Promotes transparency and accountability in program execution 5. Aligns with Government of Canada priorities for digital service delivery and open government 6. Reinforces the importance of secure access to authoritative and trusted data sources 7. Encourages a shift from siloed data access to enterprise-wide availability based on roles and responsibilities Implications 1. BDM will implement role-based access controls to ensure data is available to the right people at the right time, while maintaining security and privacy 2. Data availability will be embedded in program workflows, service design, and operational planning—not just technical systems 3. Data access policies will be aligned with privacy legislation, security standards, and departmental governance frameworks 4. Monitoring and reporting on data availability will be part of ongoing program oversight and performance management 5. BDM will promote data discoverability through cataloging and metadata to support self-service and reduce dependency on technical teams 6. Availability will be treated as a service quality metric, with feedback loops to improve access and usability over time
