BACKGROUND • In August 2019, the Government of Canada (GC) established supply contracts for Protected B Cloud Services with AWS Canada and Microsoft Azure. • For the GC to consume this supply securely and responsibly, the Chief Technology Officer of the GC deemed that an underlying operationalization framework was required. • In September 2019, the GC Cloud Operationalization Framework was approved by GC Enterprise Architecture Review Board (EARB). This included the establishment of a set of minimal security controls and architecture prior to departments consuming Pro B supply in the form of GC Cloud Guardrails. • The GC Cloud Guardrails are a set of controls focused on ensuring departments have implemented a minimum set of protections in their cloud environment focused on access management, network perimeter, segmentation, and monitoring. These Guardrails are needed in addition to a security assessment and an Authority To Operate (ATO). • In May 2022, the GC Cloud Guardrails were mandated under the Directive on Service and Digital Appendix G: Standard on Enterprise Information Technology Service Common Configurations. • In November 2022, the Office of the Auditor General’s (OAG) audit Report recommended the GC clarify the process and roles/responsibilities for validating and monitoring Cloud Guardrails and extend these to PSPC procured solutions (see Appendix A for details). • To support the Management Action Plan (the GC’s response), TBS, SSC, and PSPC initiated an activity to update the GC Cloud Guardrails.
OAG Audit of Cyber Security of Personal Information in the Cloud - KEY FINDINGS
Departmental Controls Weak in Preventing, Detecting, and Responding To Cloud Cyberattacks (Assessment)
Roles and Responsibilities for Ensuring Cloud Cybersecurity Were Unclear (Assessment)
TBS Did Not Provide A Costing Model or Funding Approach for Cloud Services (Assessment)
PSPC and SSC Did Not Include Environmental Criteria in Procurement of Cloud Services (Assessment)
OAG Audit of Cyber Security of Personal Information in the Cloud - RECOMMENDATIONS
In Consultation With SSC and PSPC, TBS Should Clarify Who Is Responsible For The Initial Validation and Ongoing Monitoring of Cloud Guardrail Controls (Course of Action)
TBS Should Ensure the GC Cyber Security Event Management Plan Applies to the Evolving Cloud Environment and Shared Responsibilities, Review and Test At Least Annually, and Update it (Course of Action)
In Consultation With SSC and Other Departments, TBS Should Develop and Provide a Costing Model to Help Make Informed Decisions About Moving To Cloud and Help Departments Determine Long-Term Operational Funding Needs (Course of Action)
OAG Audit of Cyber Security of Personal Information in the Cloud - WHY DO THE AUDIT?
Digitally Stored Information Is Exposed To Risks of Being Compromised (Driver)
GC Departments Are Moving Software Applications and Databases Into Cloud, Including Canadians’ Personal Information; Departments Must Work Together To Protect This Information From Risks, Including Cyberattacks (Driver)
Cybersecurity Breaches Are On The Rise, and Strong Controls To Prevent, Detect, and Respond To Them Can Reduce the Risk Of Breaches (Driver)
