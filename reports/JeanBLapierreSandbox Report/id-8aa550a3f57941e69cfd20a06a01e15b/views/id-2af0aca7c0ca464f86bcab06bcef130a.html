ESDC CDOB DATA PRINCIPLES (derived from the CDO Data Strategy)
P8 - Data and AI Are Used Responsibly and Ethically [Principle] Advanced analytics, automation, and AI solutions must use data responsibly, transparently, and ethically, in alignment with human rights and public expectations
P1 - Data Is an Enterprise Asset [Principle] Data created, collected, or managed by ESDC is a shared enterprise asset and not owned by a single program, system, or branch. Data must be managed for departmental value, reuse, and long-term stewardship
P3 - Data Must Be Trusted, Fit-for-Purpose, and Quality-Assured [Principle] Data must be accurate, consistent, timely, contextualized, and fit-for-purpose to support operational, policy, analytical, and service delivery needs
P10 - Data Literacy Is a Shared Responsibility [Principle] All ESDC employees share responsibility for understanding and using data appropriately according to their role, supported by enterprise data literacy initiatives
P6 - Data Sharing Is the Default, Within Legal and Ethical Boundaries [Principle] Data should be shared and reused by default across ESDC and with trusted partners when legally permissible, ethically appropriate, and aligned with departmental priorities
P2 - Data Stewardship and Accountability Are Mandatory [Principle] Every enterprise dataset must have clearly defined accountability, decision rights, and stewardship, aligned with departmental governance structures
P4 - Data Is Secure and Protected by Design [Principle] Privacy, security, legal, and ethical considerations must be embedded by design and by default throughout the data lifecycle, from collection to disposal
P7 - Data Enables Evidence-Informed and Human-Centric Decisions [Principle] Data must be used to support evidence-informed decision-making, with a human-centric focus that respects rights, inclusivity, and fairness
P5 - Data Is Discoverable and Understandable [Principle] Enterprise data assets must be discoverable, well-documented, and understandable through standard metadata, inventories, and catalogs
P9 - Data Architecture Is Standardized and Evolves Incrementally [Principle] Enterprise data architecture must be standardized, interoperable, and scalable, while evolving incrementally to support growing data maturity
